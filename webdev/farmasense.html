FarmaSense solving agricultural problem

Background

About one billion people, or better put, one out of every six persons in the sub-saharan Africa, lack access to adequate, affordable food. Hike in prices of food items and drought are forcing the population into hunger and starvation. Although, smallholder farms in sub-Saharan Africa number around 43 million, it represents 80% of all farms in the region and contribute up to 90% of food production in some sub-Saharan African Countries. But only through sustainable access to market can poor farmers increase the income from their labor and lift themselves and their families out of poverty.

How FarmaSense Works:

FarmaSense is an ideation from the farmsense project that is built on a social network approach to ease smallholder farmers and right market linkage. While doing so, we will be improving access to buyers market, access to fertilizers, access to hybrid seeds and better communication between farmers and stakeholders.

The FarmaSense solution contain a product called farmers extension toolbox, which is a system that provides a shared staged access of knowledge and information that farmers need to improve productivity and sustainability of their agricultural production value chain while increasing the farmers livelihoods. This toolbox comes in a form of free SMS service to subcribed farmers, that serves as farmer guide from the pre-planting stage through to the post-harvest stage.

FarmaSense farmers | market linkage

Rural farmer X with no Smartphone

  • Eromosele is a farmer from Auchi, a rural community in Nigeria. He sends a simple, free SMS to the local farmsense number when he is about to begin planting (Usually at the farmer's planning stage).

  • The SMS contains the number of crops he forecasts to harvest, the proposed date of harvest, the proposed cost of his products and the necessary assistance he will need (fertilizers, hybrid seeds, pesticides, labour).

  • We retrieve this information and upload it to our platform. We keep Eromosele updated while we find a buyer that wants his product.

  • During the process of finding a buyer, we give the farmer 10% of the proposed cost of his product (after verification).

    • Smallholder farmers that require financial assistance

  • Sonia sends a simple, free SMS to the local Farmsense number that she needs a particular amount of money to be able to start farming.

  • We receive this information and include Sonia in our 2 – 3 months incubation farming where we train youths and new farmers on how to farm smarter and inform them of best practices. During this period, we also carry out verification to ensure farmers that require financial aids are authenticated.

  • At the end of the incubation period, we will provide them with funds for their farming project.

    • Market

  • Musa uses our mobile/web platform to request 100 tons of cassava.

  • We collect this information and find the farmer or group of farmers that will meet Musa’s needs.

    • Changes our solution are making:

  • Faster access to knowledge and information, that farmers need to increase productivity and sustainability of farmers agricultural production value chain

  • A reliable and consistent farm produce chain(food for the season and outside the season)

  • A fast and efficient market for farmers, investors and buyers

  • A smarter and efficient supply system

  • Access to quality open data for agricultural related research and improved decision making
